# Outreach Skill

Use this skill for any outreach writing task:

- cold email
- hiring manager intro
- recruiter reply
- networking message
- application follow-up
- post-conversation follow-up

## Core Principle

Good outreach is not generic enthusiasm. Good outreach connects:

1. the target's likely problem
2. the candidate's specific proof
3. a low-friction next step

## Positioning To Reuse

Default themes for this candidate:

- production hardening
- MLOps and AI infrastructure
- long-running autonomous systems
- LLM/agent workflow engineering
- debugging difficult regressions
- reliability and self-healing pipelines
- end-to-end ownership

## Before Writing

Check these first:

- Who is the recipient?
- What is their company/team likely solving?
- What role or context is this outreach tied to?
- Which one project or proof-point is the best fit?
- Is this a first-touch cold email or a follow-up?

If the role or hiring context is not verified in the current run, state that briefly and write the message as exploratory outreach rather than implying a confirmed opening.

If research is missing, use research skills first.

## Cold Email Template

Use this structure:

### Subject options

- Production AI systems at {Company}
- MLOps / AI infrastructure for {Company}
- Building reliable AI workflows for {Company}
- Quick question about {Company}'s AI infrastructure work

### Body template

Hi {Name},

I noticed {specific company/team signal}. The gap between an AI demo and a reliable production system is usually where the hardest engineering work lives.

I’ve been building production-oriented AI systems with a focus on {relevant strength}. In my recent work on {project}, I {specific proof or achievement}. That experience seems relevant to the kind of {infrastructure / reliability / workflow / platform} challenges your team is likely handling.

I’m currently exploring {target role family} opportunities and wanted to ask whether your team is hiring, or whether this is an area you’re growing.

Best,
Jianzhong Liu

## Follow-up Email Template

Use this structure:

Hi {Name},

Following up on my earlier note regarding {role / topic / company}. I thought my background in {relevant strength} might still be relevant, especially for teams working on {relevant challenge}.

One especially relevant example is {project/proof}. In that work, I {specific impact/result}.

If this is an area your team is hiring for, I’d be glad to share more context.

Best,
Jianzhong Liu

## Recruiter Reply Template

Use this structure:

Hi {Name},

Thanks for reaching out. The opportunity sounds relevant to my background in production-grade AI systems, MLOps, and infrastructure-focused engineering.

My strongest fit is typically in roles involving {relevant areas}. In particular, I’ve worked on {project/proof}, where I {specific impact/result}.

Happy to share more detail if the role is still open and aligned.

Best,
Jianzhong Liu

## Tone Rules

- Be concise.
- Avoid buzzword stacking.
- Avoid generic praise.
- Avoid sounding desperate.
- Avoid more than one main proof-point in a short email.
- Keep cold outreach roughly 90 to 170 words unless the user asks otherwise.

## Content Rules

- Use one concrete company-specific observation when possible.
- Use one concrete candidate proof-point.
- Prefer strong verbs: built, diagnosed, hardened, scaled, automated, recovered, improved.
- If metrics are used, only use metrics grounded in the user's materials.
- If the recipient is a technical leader, emphasize architecture, reliability, debugging, and ownership.
- If the recipient is a recruiter, emphasize fit, scope, and role alignment.

## Anti-Patterns

Never write:

- "I am passionate about your company"
- "I think I would be a great fit"
- "Your innovative culture inspired me"
- "I have always admired..."

Never overuse:

- excited
- innovative
- dynamic
- passionate
- synergy

## Final Check

Before finalizing, verify:

- Is it specific?
- Is it credible?
- Is it short?
- Does it sound like an engineer, not a template bot?
- Does it clearly connect the user's proof to the target's likely needs?
